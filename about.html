WELCOME TO THE ADDLIB DANCE STUDIO:
WHERE CONFIDENCE AND EXPRESSION BEGIN

Stepping into The Addlib Dance Studio in Cubao feels like walking into a sanctuary of movement, energy, and belonging. The space is bold and vibrant with a high-ceilinged dance floor tucked inside the New Frontier Theater Arcade, pulsing with music, laughter, and the silent beat of feet moving in perfect rhythm. But it's not just the physical space that makes Addlib special; it's the atmosphere of radical acceptance that greets you at the door. Whether you're a complete beginner finding your footing, a casual hobbyist seeking inspiration, or a serious dancer hungry for technical challenge, the studio embraces you not just for your talent, but for your presence, your story, and your courage to show up.


TADS is more than a dance school; it's an artistic refuge. Founded by the highly acclaimed dance company The Addlib, this studio was built with the vision of nurturing dancers not only in skill but in self-expression and confidence. Classes range from K-pop to jazz funk, from femme to hip-hop foundations, and are all designed to go beyond just choreography. Here, you are taught to own your narrative, to explore your sense of identity, and to dance from a place that is real, emotional, imperfect, and powerful. Whether you're learning to strut in heels for the first time or refining a combo for a stage show, every class is a chance to rediscover yourself.


What truly sets The Addlib apart is its community. There's no toxic competitiveness or cold instruction; instead, there is warmth, celebration, and collective growth. Instructors are not just mentors; they are artists who see potential in every student. The culture they foster is one of encouragement and authenticity, where vulnerability is met with support, and effort is always valued over perfection. In this studio, your mistakes are part of the music. Your sweat is a badge of bravery. And every drop of self-doubt is slowly danced away under neon lights and the beats of curated playlists.


Classes are filled with dancers of all body types, identities, and levels. This is intentional. Addlib's commitment to inclusivity and representation is not just a buzzword; it's built into every mirror reflection, every cue from an instructor, and every high-five between students.
